Frequently Asked Questions

What is an urban heat island?

Urban heat islands are urbanized areas that experience higher temperatures than outlying areas. Structures such as buildings, roads, and other infrastructure absorb and re-emit the sun's heat more than natural landscapes, causing urban areas to become "islands" of higher temperatures.

How does the GreenGrids work?

Our tool uses satellite thermal imagery, road and rooftop data, and environmental insights to identify and analyze urban heat islands. It then provides data-driven recommendations for strategic greening interventions, such as tree planting locations, to maximize cooling effects.

Can you analyze any city or urban area?

Yes, our technology can be applied to cities and urban areas of any size around the world. We can provide solutions at both the city-wide level and for specific neighborhoods or districts.

How accurate is the temperature and green coverage data?

The accuracy of our data depends on the specific product and the quality of available satellite imagery, but generally ranges from 75-95% accuracy. Our City-Wide Heat Maps have an accuracy of approximately 75%, while our detailed Local Heat Maps and Cooling Potential Maps have accuracy ratings of up to 95%.

How can cities use this information?

Cities can use our tools to develop targeted climate adaptation strategies, prioritize green infrastructure investments, improve public health outcomes during heat waves, reduce energy consumption for cooling, and create more livable urban environments. The data and recommendations can inform urban planning, parks and recreation management, and climate action plans.
